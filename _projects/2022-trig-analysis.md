1. PROBLEM STATEMENT AND OBJECTIVE

The objective of this project is to determine viable dimensions for a hand-operated simple lever nutcracker capable of amplifying an induced force to crack a macadamia nut.

2. CONSTRAINTS AND INPUT PARAMETERS

Required Force (RF) = 2500 N; 
Nut Diameter = 30 mm; 
Input Force (IF) = 350 N (based on average human grip force); 
Lrf = 20 mm

3. APPROACH

5. DISCUSSION

This nutcracker design provides a mechanical advantage of approximately 7.14, allowing a user applying a grip force of about 350 N to generate roughly 2500 N at the nut. This output force is sufficient to crack a macadamia nut, which typically requires between 1800 and 4000 N to fracture. Therefore, the device should be capable of cracking most macadamia nuts with a single squeeze. 

The nut placement distance of approximately 20 mm from the pivot creates the required mechanical advantage but also introduces some limitations. Because the crushing force increases as the nut is placed closer to the pivot, users may need to position harder nuts deeper in the jaw region.
